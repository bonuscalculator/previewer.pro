// writing tips

Posts that get engagement.

01

Hook in the first line

The first few lines are what show in the feed preview. Put the most compelling statement right at the start.

02

Keep paragraphs short

Dense blocks get skimmed. Break up your post into short, scannable paragraphs for better readability.

03

Use a high-quality image

Images with clear focal points perform better. Use the preview to ensure your crop isn't cutting off important details.

04

// faq

Questions, answered plainly.

What is the character limit for a Facebook post?+

Facebook posts can be up to 63,206 characters. This tool counts characters live and warns you as you approach the limit.

Can I preview an image with my Facebook post?+

Yes. Upload an image and it will appear inside the mockup exactly where it would in a real post, so you can check the crop and composition.

Can I preview a link on Facebook?+

Yes. Toggle to link mode and fill in the URL, title, description, and upload an image to see how the link preview will render in the feed.

Does this tool post to Facebook for me?+

No. This is a preview-only tool. It never connects to your Facebook account and never publishes anything on your behalf.

Can I see how my post looks in dark mode?+

Yes. Use the light/dark toggle above the preview to check how your post renders in both of Facebook's display modes.

Is my post text or image stored anywhere?+

Your draft text is saved only in your own browser's local storage so it survives a refresh. Uploaded images are not saved and nothing is sent to a server.
