// why this matters

Small things that break a tweet.

The 280-character wall

Go one character over and your post won't send. The live ring counter shows exactly how much room you have left as you type.

Awkward image crops

X crops images to fit the feed. Uploading here shows the same aspect ratio so you catch a bad crop before it's public.

Light vs. dark mode

A big chunk of your audience reads X in dark mode. Toggle the preview to make sure your image and emoji still read well on black.

// writing tips

Tweets that don't get lost in the scroll.

01

Front-load the point

The first line is what shows in notifications and quote-tweets. Put the actual point there, not the setup.

02

One image, one idea

A single, well-cropped image usually reads clearer than a busy collage. Preview it here before you decide.

03

Leave room for quote-tweets

If people are likely to quote you, keep your post under ~250 characters so their comment doesn't feel cramped.

04

Check it in dark mode

Light text on a transparent image can vanish on a black background. The dark toggle catches this in seconds.

// faq

Questions, answered plainly.

What is the character limit for a post on X?+

Standard posts on X are limited to 280 characters. This tool counts characters live and warns you when you go over the limit.

Can I preview an image with my tweet?+

Yes. Upload an image and it will appear inside the mockup exactly where it would in a real post, so you can check the crop and composition.

Does this tool post to X for me?+

No. This is a preview-only tool. It never connects to your X account and never publishes anything on your behalf.

Can I see how my post looks in dark mode?+

Yes. Use the light/dark toggle above the preview to check how your post renders in both of X's display modes.

Is my tweet text stored anywhere?+

Your draft text is saved only in your own browser's local storage so it survives a refresh. Uploaded images are not saved and nothing is sent to a server.
