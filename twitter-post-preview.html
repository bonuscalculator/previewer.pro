Card Types

Twitter Card Types Explained

Choosing the right card type dramatically affects how your shared links perform on Twitter/X.

🖼️

Summary Large Image

The most engaging card type. Displays a prominent image (min 300×157px, max 4096×4096px, up to 5MB) above the title and description. Ideal for blog posts, articles, and landing pages.

twitter:card = summary_large_image
📋

Summary Card

Shows a small thumbnail (144×144px minimum) to the left of the title and description. Works best for product pages, documentation, or any page where the image is secondary to the text.

twitter:card = summary
📱

App Card

Specifically designed for promoting iOS and Android apps. Displays app name, icon, rating, and price alongside a download button. Requires Apple App Store or Google Play IDs in meta tags.

twitter:card = app
Best Practices

8 Tips to Maximise Twitter Card CTR

01

Use 1200×628px Images

This is the optimal Open Graph image size that works across all platforms including Twitter/X. Avoid text near the edges which may be cropped on smaller viewports.

02

Keep Titles Under 70 Characters

Twitter truncates titles at around 70 characters. Front-load the most important keywords and value proposition so nothing critical is cut off.

03

Write Descriptions Under 200 Characters

Twitter shows approximately 200 characters of the description. Use the space to add a compelling reason to click that complements the title.

04

Always Specify twitter:card

Without this tag, Twitter won't generate a card. Even if you have og:image set, you need explicit twitter:card to trigger card rendering.

05

Use a Dedicated OG Image

Don't use your site logo or a generic image. Create page-specific OG images that reflect the content — articles should show the headline, products should show the product.

06

Test Before Every Campaign

Twitter caches card data. Use our preview tool before scheduling posts, and use Twitter's Card Validator to clear the cache after updating meta tags.

07

Add twitter:site and twitter:creator

Including your Twitter/X handle in twitter:site links the card to your account, making it easier for viewers to follow you directly from the card.

08

Validate Your Image URL

The image URL must be absolute (not relative), publicly accessible, and return a 200 status code. CDN-hosted images with proper CORS headers work best.

What Are Twitter Cards and Why Do They Matter?

Twitter Cards are structured rich media experiences attached to a tweet whenever someone shares a URL. When you share a link on Twitter/X, the platform fetches the page's meta tags and constructs a card — an image, title, description, and domain attribution — that appears directly below the tweet text in the feed.

Without a properly configured Twitter Card, your link appears as plain text. With one, it becomes a visual, clickable card that stands out in a busy feed and drives significantly higher engagement and click-through rates.

How to Add Twitter Card Meta Tags

Twitter Card meta tags belong in the <head> of your HTML page. The minimum required tags for a Summary Large Image card are:

<!-- Required for all cards -->
<meta name="twitter:card" content="summary_large_image" />
<meta name="twitter:title" content="Your Page Title Here" />
<meta name="twitter:description" content="Your page description here." />
<meta name="twitter:image" content="https://your-site.com/og-image.jpg" />

<!-- Recommended -->
<meta name="twitter:site" content="@yourtwitterhandle" />
<meta name="twitter:creator" content="@authorhandle" />

The Relationship Between OG Tags and Twitter Cards

Twitter/X will fall back to Open Graph tags (og:title, og:description, og:image) if Twitter-specific tags are not present. However, it's best practice to set both. Twitter Card tags give you more control and ensure the card renders exactly as intended, while OG tags cover other platforms like Facebook and LinkedIn simultaneously.

Twitter Card Image Requirements

For summary_large_image: minimum 300×157px, recommended 1200×628px, maximum file size 5MB, supported formats: JPG, PNG, WebP, GIF (first frame only). For summary: minimum 144×144px, recommended 400×400px. Images must be absolute URLs and publicly accessible without authentication.
