// why serp preview

Why SERP previews matter for SEO.

First impression counts

Your SERP snippet is the first thing users see. A well-optimized title and description can increase click-through rates by up to 30%.

Avoid truncation

Google truncates titles over 60 characters and descriptions over 160 characters. Preview your snippet to ensure your full message is visible.

Mobile vs. desktop

Mobile SERPs show fewer characters. Use the mobile toggle to see exactly how your snippet will appear on smartphones.

// faq

Questions, answered plainly.

What is a SERP preview?+

A SERP (Search Engine Results Page) preview shows how your page will appear in Google search results. It includes the title tag, meta description, and URL displayed in the search results.

What are the ideal character limits for SEO?+

For Google search results, titles should be 50-60 characters and meta descriptions should be 150-160 characters. Google may truncate longer text, so staying within these limits ensures your full content is visible.

How does Google generate the breadcrumb path?+

Google generates breadcrumbs from your page URL structure or from schema.org BreadcrumbList markup. It shows a simplified path that helps users understand where the page belongs in your site hierarchy.

Does this tool connect to Google?+

No. This is a preview-only tool that simulates how Google displays search results. It doesn't connect to Google's API or affect your actual search ranking.

Can I preview mobile SERP results?+

Yes. Use the desktop/mobile toggle to see how your search result will appear on mobile devices, which often show slightly different formatting and truncation.

Does this tool validate my schema markup?+

No. This tool only previews the visual appearance of your SERP snippet. For schema validation, use Google's Rich Results Test tool.
