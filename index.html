Frequently Asked Questions

A mobile website viewer is an online tool that renders your URL inside a simulated device frame — such as an iPhone 15 or Samsung Galaxy S24 — at the exact viewport size of that device. It uses an iframe or screenshot engine to load your page at the correct screen resolution, letting you inspect layout, typography, images, and navigation without needing a physical device or emulator.
Our social media preview tool reads the same Open Graph, Twitter Card, and structured data meta tags that social platforms read when you share a URL. The preview renders these tags into pixel-accurate card mockups that closely match how each platform actually displays shared links. Minor visual differences may exist when platforms change their card templates, which we update promptly.
Our SERP preview tool simulates Google's rendering logic, including pixel-width-based truncation for titles (600px) and descriptions (920px). However, Google may also rewrite titles and descriptions dynamically based on the search query and page content. Our tool reflects what Google will show when using your specified tags directly — it's the most accurate pre-publish check available.
Yes, all three tools — Mobile Viewer, Social Media Preview, and SERP Preview — are completely free to use with no account required. There are no usage limits during the free tier. We believe essential web publishing tools should be accessible to everyone.
For the social media and SERP preview tools, the URL needs to be publicly accessible since our servers fetch your meta tags server-side. The mobile viewer uses an iframe, so local URLs like localhost may work depending on your browser and network setup. For secure staging environments, you can use tools like ngrok to temporarily expose your local server.
Our social media post preview tool currently supports Facebook (link cards), Twitter/X (summary and summary_large_image cards), LinkedIn (rich link posts), WhatsApp (URL previews), Telegram (link previews), and Pinterest (rich pins). We regularly add new platforms as social sharing behaviour evolves.
