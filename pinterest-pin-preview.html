💡 Pinterest Pin Tips

📐

Always Use 2:3 Ratio

The 2:3 aspect ratio (1000×1500px) takes up the most vertical feed space — up to 67% higher CTR than square images.

🔑

Keywords in Title

Pinterest is a visual search engine. Put your primary keyword in the first 30 characters of the title for maximum discoverability.

📌

Enable Rich Pins

Rich Pins pull real-time metadata from your site. They show more info in search and are far more clickable than standard pins.

🔗

Always Link to a Page

Pins that link to specific content (not homepage) convert 8× better. Use UTM parameters to track traffic.

❓ FAQs

The recommended size is 1000×1500px at a 2:3 aspect ratio. This format occupies the most vertical space in the Pinterest feed, maximising visibility. Pinterest also accepts 1:1 and 9:16 formats, but the 2:3 ratio consistently delivers the highest engagement and click-through rates.
Add Schema.org markup (for Product, Recipe, or Article Rich Pins) or Open Graph tags to your page. Then use Pinterest's Rich Pin Validator to verify your markup and activate Rich Pins for your domain. Once approved, all pins from your site automatically become Rich Pins.
