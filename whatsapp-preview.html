// best practices

Optimize your WhatsApp link previews.

01

Use og:image

WhatsApp prioritizes og:image. Use an image that's at least 200×200px with a 1.91:1 aspect ratio for best results.

02

Keep titles concise

WhatsApp shows about 60-70 characters of the title. Put your most important message in the first 60 characters.

03

Write clear descriptions

Descriptions under 150 characters perform best. Preview here to see exactly how much shows up in the card.

04

Use absolute image URLs

WhatsApp requires absolute URLs (starting with https://) for images. Relative paths or localhost URLs won't work.

// faq

Questions, answered plainly.

How does WhatsApp generate link previews?+

WhatsApp uses Open Graph meta tags (og:title, og:description, og:image) from the page to generate previews. If no OG tags are found, it falls back to page title and a generic preview.

What meta tags does WhatsApp use?+

WhatsApp primarily uses og:title, og:description, and og:image. The image should be at least 200×200px for best results, with 1.91:1 aspect ratio recommended.

Does this tool send my URL to WhatsApp?+

No. This is a preview-only tool. It fetches the page metadata to display a preview, but never sends anything to WhatsApp or publishes on your behalf.

Can I test a localhost URL?+

No. The tool requires publicly accessible URLs to fetch meta tags. Localhost or internal network URLs cannot be reached by the proxy service.

Is my URL stored anywhere?+

No. All fetching and previewing happens in your browser. The URL and meta tags are never stored on any server.
