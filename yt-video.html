// writing tips

Videos that get clicks.

01

Front-load the hook

The first few words of the title are what show in search. Put the most compelling keyword or phrase right up front.

02

Keep it under 60 characters

While the limit is 100, shorter titles often perform better. Preview how it looks in the mockup to see if it's scannable.

03

Thumbnail readability

Text on thumbnails should be large and high-contrast. Upload your thumbnail and check it in both light and dark mode.

04

Check channel avatar

Your channel avatar appears next to the video. Make sure it's recognizable and fits the context.

// faq

Questions, answered plainly.

What is the character limit for a YouTube video title?+

YouTube video titles are limited to 100 characters. This tool counts characters live and warns you as you approach the limit.

Can I preview my thumbnail with the title?+

Yes. Upload a thumbnail image and it will appear inside the mockup exactly where it would in YouTube search results.

Does this tool upload my video to YouTube?+

No. This is a preview-only tool. It never connects to your YouTube account and never publishes anything on your behalf.

Can I see how my video looks in dark mode?+

Yes. Use the light/dark toggle above the preview to check how your video card renders in both of YouTube's display modes.

Is my video title or thumbnail stored anywhere?+

Your draft title is saved only in your own browser's local storage so it survives a refresh. Uploaded thumbnails are not saved and nothing is sent to a server.
